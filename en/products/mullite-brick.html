Mullite Brick Overview

Mullite bricks are premium refractory materials based on the mineral mullite (3Al₂O₃·2SiO₂). They offer exceptional thermal shock resistance, high mechanical strength, and excellent chemical stability, making them ideal for extreme high-temperature applications.

Mullite Crystal Structure

The unique properties of mullite bricks stem from the mullite crystal structure:

  • Primary Mullite: Natural mullite crystals formed from raw materials
  • Secondary Mullite: Mullite formed during firing process
  • Fused Mullite: High-purity mullite from electric fusion
  • Sintered Mullite: Dense mullite from controlled sintering

Manufacturing Excellence

Our mullite bricks are manufactured using advanced technology:

  • Selection of high-purity mullite, alumina, and sillimanite raw materials
  • Precise control of Al₂O₃/SiO₂ ratio for optimal properties
  • Advanced mixing and granulation techniques
  • High-pressure molding for maximum density
  • Controlled firing at temperatures up to 1700℃
  • Comprehensive quality control and testing

Superior Performance Characteristics

  • Exceptional Thermal Shock Resistance: Superior to most refractory materials
  • High Refractoriness: Service temperatures up to 1700℃
  • Low Thermal Expansion: Minimal dimensional changes during heating
  • High Mechanical Strength: Excellent strength retention at elevated temperatures
  • Chemical Stability: Resistance to most slags and chemical attack
  • Creep Resistance: Maintains shape under load at high temperatures
  • Thermal Conductivity: Good heat transfer properties

Quality Assurance Program

Every batch of mullite bricks undergoes rigorous testing:

  • Physical property measurements

Grades and Selection

We offer multiple grades to meet specific application requirements:

  • Economy Grade: Cost-effective solution for less demanding applications

Installation Guidelines

For optimal performance, consider these installation practices:

  • Allow for thermal expansion in joint design
  • Follow recommended heating schedules
  • Ensure proper backing and support
