High Alumina Brick Overview

High alumina bricks are premium refractory materials containing 55% or more Al₂O₃. They offer superior performance compared to clay bricks, featuring higher refractoriness, better corrosion resistance, and excellent mechanical strength at elevated temperatures.

Manufacturing Process

Our high alumina bricks are manufactured using state-of-the-art technology:

  • Selection of premium-grade bauxite and alumina raw materials
  • Precise calcination to achieve optimal crystal structure
  • Advanced mixing and grinding techniques
  • High-pressure molding for superior density
  • Controlled firing at temperatures up to 1650℃
  • Comprehensive quality testing and certification

Key Advantages

  • High Refractoriness: Service temperatures up to 1790℃ and beyond
  • Superior Strength: Exceptional mechanical strength at high temperatures
  • Chemical Resistance: Excellent resistance to acidic and basic slags
  • Thermal Stability: Outstanding thermal shock resistance
  • Low Porosity: Reduced infiltration and longer service life
  • Dimensional Stability: Minimal thermal expansion and shrinkage

Quality Control

Each batch of high alumina bricks undergoes rigorous testing:

  • X-ray fluorescence (XRF) chemical analysis
  • Physical property measurement (porosity, density, strength)
  • Thermal property evaluation (refractoriness, thermal expansion)
  • Corrosion resistance testing
  • Dimensional tolerance verification
  • Microstructure analysis

Grades Available

We offer multiple grades to meet different application requirements:

  • HA-85: Ultra-high alumina content for extreme conditions
  • HA-75: High-performance grade for demanding applications
  • HA-65: Standard high alumina for general high-temperature use
  • HA-55: Cost-effective solution for moderate conditions
